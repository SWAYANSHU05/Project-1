TOP 3 UPPER MOON DEMONS OF TAISHO ERA

K O K U S H I B O (UPPER MOON 1)

Kokushibo, known in his human life as Michikatsu Tsugikuni, is the Upper Moon One demon in the Demon Slayer series and a central antagonist within the story. Born during the Sengoku era as the older twin brother of Yoriichi Tsugikuni—the progenitor of all Breathing Styles and the strongest Demon Slayer in history—Kokushibo was raised in privilege and luxury, yet lived in the constant shadow of his younger brother’s extraordinary talent and the mysterious Demon Slayer Mark that Yoriichi bore. This deep-seated jealousy and fear of mediocrity drove him to seek power beyond human limits. Despite being a skilled swordsman and respected member of the Demon Slayer Corps, Kokushibo felt his worth diminished by Yoriichi’s unmatched strength and the divine favor he received. When he discovered he had also gained the Demon Slayer Mark, he was told it would curse him to die by the age of 25, a fate he could not accept. In desperation, he met Muzan Kibutsuji, who offered him immortality and the chance to surpass his brother by becoming a demon. This decision marked his descent into darkness, as he willingly abandoned his family, his humanity, and his moral compass to pursue strength and validation through demonic power. As a demon, Kokushibo underwent a dramatic transformation: he gained six eyes—three pairs, with the central pair bearing the kanji for "Upper Moon One"—and his hair turned black with red tips, tied in a ponytail. He developed the Moon Breathing technique, a style derived from Sun Breathing, which he created out of envy and a desire to rival his brother’s legacy. He became the most powerful demon under Muzan, serving as his right-hand man and holding the rank of Upper Moon One for nearly 500 years. He defeated multiple challengers for his position, absorbing their power to grow stronger, and was known for his cold, silent, and aloof demeanor, often sitting in silence with an aura of unnerving tranquility. Kokushibo’s obsession with surpassing Yoriichi led him to betray his brother and the Demon Slayer Corps, a betrayal that ultimately contributed to Yoriichi’s downfall and loss of favor among other Demon Slayers. His life was defined by psychological torment, insecurity, and a relentless pursuit of superiority, which ultimately caused his downfall. During the Infinity Castle Arc, he was finally defeated by the combined efforts of Muichiro Tokito, Sanemi and Genya Shinazugawa, and Gyomei Himejima. His death marked the end of the Twelve Kizuki and left Muzan as the last remaining demon, though he had already lost his will to live after seeing the monstrous form he had become. Kokushibo is often portrayed as a stoic, powerful, and emotionally reserved figure, with a deep connection to his past and a complex relationship with his brother Yoriichi. He is frequently depicted as a central figure in stories involving the Infinity Castle, his rivalry with other Upper Moons, and his interactions with original characters or alternate versions of himself. characters often emphasize his cold demeanor, six eyes, and mastery of Moon Breathing, while allowing users to explore alternate narratives such as being his daughter, a fellow demon, or even a human who survives his presence. Kokushibo’s tragic backstory—rooted in envy, loss, and the tragic choice to become a monster—has made him one of the most complex and emotionally resonant villains in the series, often described as one of the saddest and most evil characters in Demon Slayer.

D O M A (UPPER MOON 2)

Doma, also known as Upper Moon Two in the Twelve Kizuki, is a powerful and complex antagonist in the Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba series, portrayed as a high-functioning psychopath with a cheerful and charismatic facade masking his profound emotional detachment. Born with unique rainbow-colored eyes and pale hair, Doma was exploited by his parents, who used his appearance to establish a cult dedicated to worshipping him as a divine being. This false divinity was a source of immense power and attention, but Doma himself felt no genuine emotions, viewing his followers as pitiful worms begging for mercy, and he never truly cared for them, even during their suffering. Despite being raised in a position of god-like reverence, Doma was emotionally stunted from birth, lacking the capacity for human emotions such as joy, sadness, anger, or pleasure. His parents' deaths—his mother killing his father after discovering his infidelity, followed by her suicide—did not affect him; he only regretted the mess left behind. This emotional void led him to mimic human feelings, creating a carefully crafted, cheerful demeanor that he uses to manipulate and deceive others. He is known for calling demons "Dono" (e.g., Akaza-Dono), a sign of his formal yet condescending respect, and he refers to humans as "Poor Little Thing". Doma's Blood Demon Art is cryokinesis, allowing him to generate and manipulate ice to create constructs such as frost lotuses, stalagmites, clouds of icy air, and even miniature ice clones. He wields two golden fans engraved with lotus designs as his primary weapons, using them with deadly precision in combat. He is also capable of absorbing human bodies and possesses immense speed and regeneration, typical of demons. As a demon, Doma is loyal to Muzan Kibutsuji, the King of Demons, and serves as one of the strongest demons in the hierarchy. He is known for his disturbingly selective diet—he only consumes women, believing they contain more nutrients due to their role in nurturing babies, while he kills men. He views his consumption of followers not as murder, but as a form of salvation, claiming he is freeing them from suffering. This twisted philosophy is rooted in his belief that he is above humanity, which he pities for its weakness. Doma is the leader of the Eternal Paradise Faith, a cult that serves both as a place for him to gather and kill victims and as a space where he listens to followers' troubles, offering advice while secretly planning their demise. He is also known for his strange, one-sided friendship with Akaza, the Upper Moon Three, whom he considers his best friend despite Akaza's clear hatred for him. This dynamic highlights Doma's inability to understand genuine human relationships, as he interprets affection through his own distorted lens. Doma is often depicted as charming, possessive, and manipulative, with a tendency to fall in love with women he encounters, sometimes sparing them if he finds them entertaining or beautiful, his ability to fake emotions, and his sadistic tendencies, often placing users in scenarios where they must navigate his dangerous charm. Overall, Doma stands out as one of the most disturbing and psychologically complex antagonists in Demon Slayer, embodying a chilling blend of intelligence, charisma, and emotional emptiness.


A K A Z A (UPPER MOON 3)

Akaza, formerly known as Hakuji, is a major antagonist in the Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba franchise, serving as the Upper Rank Three within the Twelve Kizuki under Muzan Kibutsuji. His life is defined by profound tragedy and a relentless pursuit of strength, beginning as a young man in the Edo period who resorted to theft to afford medicine for his ailing father. After his father's suicide, driven by guilt over his son's suffering, Hakuji was exiled and fell into rage, eventually finding purpose under the mentorship of Keizo, a martial artist who took him in to care for his sick daughter, Koyuki. The bond he formed with Koyuki and Keizo brought him fleeting happiness, but this was shattered when a rival dojo poisoned them, leading Hakuji to massacre 67 members of the rival dojo in a fit of grief and rage. This act of violence caught Muzan's attention, who offered Hakuji immortality as a demon, a choice made out of despair and the loss of everything he loved. As Akaza, he lost his human memories but retained a deep-seated moral code, refusing to harm or eat women, a trait that distinguished him among the demons. His final battle against Tanjiro Kamado and Giyu Tomioka triggered the return of his repressed memories, leading to a moment of profound regret and self-realization. Overwhelmed by guilt for his actions and the lives he destroyed, Akaza chose to end his own life, seeking peace in the afterlife with the loved ones he failed to protect. Origins and Human Life: Hakuji was born into poverty in the slums of Edo, where he stole to afford medicine for his sick father. After being repeatedly punished and branded as a "demon child" by authorities, his father took his own life, leaving a letter urging Hakuji to live an honest life. Banished and consumed by rage, Hakuji nearly killed seven men before being taken in by Keizo, who trained him in the Soryu style and entrusted him with caring for his daughter, Koyuki. Tragedy and Transformation: Hakuji developed deep feelings for Koyuki, and Keizo offered him the chance to take over the dojo and marry her. However, upon returning from his father’s grave, he discovered that Keizo and Koyuki had been poisoned by a rival dojo. In a blind rage, he killed all 67 members of the rival dojo with his bare hands, an act so brutal that Muzan Kibutsuji himself was impressed and offered him the chance to become a demon. Demon Life and Abilities: As Akaza, he lost his memories of his human life but retained an unyielding desire to become stronger, a drive that defined his existence. He became one of the strongest demons in the series, defeating the Flame Hashira Kyojuro Rengoku and other Hashira. Despite his immense power, his refusal to eat women limited his growth, yet he still achieved the rank of Upper Rank Three. His combat style, based on the Soryu style, combined raw speed, power, and predictive movement, making him nearly impossible to hit. Final Battle and Redemption: During the Infinity Castle arc, Akaza fought Tanjiro and Giyu Tomioka. After being beheaded, his human memories resurfaced, showing him visions of his father, Keizo, and Koyuki. He realized the monster he had become and, overwhelmed by regret, chose to end his life through seppuku, finally finding peace in the afterlife.
